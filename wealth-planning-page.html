Our Approach

How we build your wealth plan

We don't hand you a PDF and disappear. Your plan is a living document we review and refine with you every year.

1

Deep Discovery

A thorough conversation about your life, values, goals, and fears. We want to understand you before we ever open a spreadsheet.

2

Financial Audit

We review every account, policy, document, and tax return to get a complete picture of where things stand today.

3

Plan Construction

We build a comprehensive written plan covering investments, taxes, protection, and estate — with specific recommendations and timelines.

4

Implementation

We put the plan into motion — opening accounts, restructuring investments, coordinating with attorneys and CPAs, and executing on every action item.

5

Annual Reviews & Updates

Life changes. Markets change. Tax laws change. We meet with you every year to review, update, and keep your plan aligned with your life.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Honest answers to the questions we hear most.

What's the difference between wealth planning and investment management?
+
Investment management is one component of wealth planning. A wealth plan encompasses your entire financial life — goals, cash flow, taxes, insurance, estate, and investments — all working together. Think of investment management as the engine and wealth planning as the whole car.
How much does comprehensive planning cost?
+
We charge a fee based on assets under management, which aligns our incentives with yours — we do better when you do better. We're happy to walk through our fee structure in detail during a no-obligation consultation.
How often will I hear from my advisor?
+
At minimum, we meet with every planning client annually for a full plan review. Most clients also connect with us quarterly, and we proactively reach out during major market events or life changes. You can always call or email your advisor directly — no call centers.
Do I need to bring all my accounts to Greenfield?
+
Not necessarily. We can provide comprehensive planning advice even if we don't manage all your assets. That said, consolidating accounts often leads to better coordination and lower overall costs. We'll give you an honest recommendation based on your situation.
