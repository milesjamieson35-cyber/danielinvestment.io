What We Do

Tax planning, not just tax preparation

Most people only think about taxes in April. We think about your taxes every month — looking for opportunities to reduce what you owe today and in the future.

🔄

Roth Conversion Planning

Converting pre-tax retirement assets to Roth in low-income years can dramatically reduce your lifetime tax bill. We model the optimal conversion amount each year.

📉

Tax-Loss Harvesting

We systematically identify losses in your taxable accounts that can offset capital gains — reducing your annual tax bill without meaningfully changing your investment strategy.

🏦

Account Location Strategy

Placing the right investments in the right account types (taxable, traditional, Roth) can significantly improve your after-tax returns over time.

📊

Capital Gains Management

We time the realization of capital gains and losses, coordinate with charitable giving, and use other strategies to minimize taxable events each year.

CPA Coordination

🏢

Business Owner Tax Planning

Entity structure, deferred compensation, retirement plan contributions, and exit planning — tax strategy for business owners requires a specialized approach.

Our Approach

Year-round, not just year-end

Reactive tax planning leaves money on the table. We build tax strategy into every financial decision we make on your behalf throughout the year.

1

Tax Situation Analysis

We review your recent tax returns to understand your current bracket, income sources, deductions, and year-over-year trends — identifying immediate opportunities.

2

Multi-Year Tax Projection

We model your tax picture 5–10 years out, accounting for retirement, Social Security, RMDs, and estate — so we can make decisions today that benefit you for decades.

3

Strategy Implementation

We execute tax-saving strategies throughout the year — Roth conversions, loss harvesting, asset location adjustments, and charitable giving coordination.

4

Year-End Tax Review

Each fall we run a tax projection for the current year and make any final adjustments — additional conversions, harvesting opportunities, or charitable contributions — before December 31.

5

CPA Handoff & Filing Support

We provide your CPA with a detailed summary of all investment activity, tax elections made, and planning notes to ensure your return is filed accurately and efficiently.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Answers to the tax questions we hear most often.

Are you a CPA? Do you prepare tax returns?
+
We are financial advisors who specialize in tax strategy and planning — not tax preparers. We work alongside your CPA (or refer you to one) to ensure your investment and financial decisions are made with a clear view of their tax consequences. Think of us as the strategic layer above the tax return.
What's the difference between tax planning and tax preparation?
+
Tax preparation is reporting what happened last year. Tax planning is making decisions throughout the year to change what will happen next year. Preparation looks backward; planning looks forward. Most of the value — and most of the savings — comes from planning, not preparation.
Should I do a Roth conversion?
+
For many clients in their 50s and early 60s — after their peak earning years but before Social Security and RMDs begin — strategic Roth conversions can save significant taxes over a lifetime. The right answer depends on your current bracket, projected future income, and estate goals. We model this individually for every client.
How much can tax planning realistically save me?
+
It varies widely depending on your income level, account types, and situation. For clients with $1–5M in assets, proactive tax planning often saves $10,000–$30,000 per year when you account for Roth conversions, loss harvesting, asset location, and charitable strategies. For high earners or business owners, the savings can be substantially higher.
