Our Approach

How we build your retirement plan

We don't use a generic template. Every retirement plan starts with a deep understanding of your life — your goals, your concerns, and what retirement actually means to you.

1

Discovery & Goal Setting

We start with a conversation — not a spreadsheet. We want to understand what your ideal retirement looks like: where you'll live, what you'll do, and what keeps you up at night. From there we define the financial targets that make that life possible.

2

Retirement Income Modeling

We build a detailed cash flow model projecting your income sources, expenses, and portfolio through age 95. You'll see exactly how your plan performs in bull markets, bear markets, and every scenario in between.

3

Tax & Withdrawal Optimization

We map out the most tax-efficient sequence to draw from your accounts each year, coordinate Roth conversions, and structure required minimum distributions to minimize your lifetime tax bill.

4

Risk & Protection Review

We assess your exposure to longevity risk, sequence-of-returns risk, and healthcare costs — then recommend appropriate safeguards including insurance, annuities (when warranted), and reserve strategies.

5

Ongoing Annual Reviews

Your retirement plan is a living document. We review it with you every year — adjusting for tax law changes, market performance, and any life events that shift your priorities or timeline.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Questions we hear from nearly every client — answered clearly.

How much do I need to retire comfortably?
+
The traditional "25x your annual expenses" rule is a useful starting point, but the real answer depends on your specific retirement age, lifestyle, healthcare needs, and income sources like Social Security or a pension. We build personalized cash flow models for every client rather than relying on generic benchmarks.
When should I claim Social Security?
+
This is one of the most consequential decisions in retirement — and it depends on your health, other income sources, marital status, and tax situation. Claiming at 62 vs. 70 can mean a difference of 76% in your monthly benefit. We run a full Social Security analysis for every retirement planning client.
What's the best order to withdraw from my accounts?
+
Generally, it makes sense to draw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts (like a traditional IRA or 401k), and let Roth accounts grow tax-free for as long as possible. But this varies significantly based on your tax bracket, RMD timeline, and estate goals — which is why a personalized strategy matters so much.
What if the market drops right when I retire?
+
This is called sequence-of-returns risk — and it's one of the biggest threats to a retirement plan. We address it through cash reserve strategies, flexible withdrawal rates, and portfolio construction designed to weather early-retirement downturns without permanently damaging your income stream.
Do I need long-term care insurance?
+
It depends on your assets, family situation, and risk tolerance. Long-term care costs can easily exceed $100,000 per year — and Medicare doesn't cover most of it. We evaluate whether insurance makes financial sense for you or whether self-insuring through a dedicated reserve is more appropriate.
