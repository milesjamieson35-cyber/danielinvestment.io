Our Process

How we conduct your risk review

We approach insurance as financial advisors — objectively and in your interest — not as agents with products to sell.

1

Coverage Inventory

We gather all of your existing policies — life, disability, long-term care, umbrella, property, and business — and create a comprehensive inventory of what you have today.

2

Needs Analysis

We calculate your actual coverage needs based on income, liabilities, dependents, assets, and risk exposure — and compare that to what you currently carry.

3

Gap & Redundancy Identification

We identify where you're exposed and where you may be over-paying for coverage you no longer need — and quantify the financial impact of each gap.

4

Recommendations & Market Comparison

Where new or replacement coverage is warranted, we help you compare options across the market and find the most cost-effective solution for your needs.

5

Annual Review

Your coverage needs change as your life changes. We review your protection picture every year and flag when updates are needed.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Plain answers to the insurance questions we hear most.

Do you sell insurance products?
+
Our primary role is as objective financial advisors. When our analysis identifies a coverage need, we help you understand your options and can facilitate the process of obtaining coverage — but our recommendation is always based on your best interest, not on earning a commission. We're transparent about any compensation structure involved.
How much life insurance do I actually need?
+
The old "10x income" rule is a rough starting point, but the right answer depends on your debts, income replacement needs, education funding goals, surviving spouse's earning capacity, and estate plan. We build a specific needs analysis for every client rather than relying on rules of thumb.
Should I buy term or permanent life insurance?
+
For most people with a straightforward need — protecting income and replacing human capital during working years — term insurance is the most cost-effective solution. Permanent insurance can make sense in specific estate planning, business, or tax-deferral situations. We'll give you an honest recommendation based on your actual circumstances.
When should I start thinking about long-term care coverage?
+
The ideal window to obtain long-term care insurance is typically between ages 50 and 65 — after you've accumulated assets worth protecting but before health conditions make coverage expensive or unavailable. Waiting too long is the most common mistake. We evaluate this as part of every comprehensive retirement planning engagement.
